Homecourt Rewards Store

Rewarding the Hardworking Players

The AI-powered basketball training app is now seeking to make basketball training experience even more exciting & rewarding

/ Client Brief

'We want to boost retention especially from kids.'

The app is already very well-known worldwide. Being adopted in training NBA, professional and school teams. However, the retention rate among individual users is still a large piece of puzzle for Homecourt to consider

/ Insights

'Who as a NBA fan doesn't play NBA on a console?'

As a kid who is also a NBA fan, he/she must have played NBA on consoles for at least once in their life time. Why don't we further enhance the virtual-to-real world training experience by offering them a game-like reward system?

/ Strategies

Foster & enhance the fan-identity

Basketball fans are often passionate on everything about their supporting team. We allow them to build up a Homecourt profile full of their supporting team's visuals

Collect & show-off

Unlike any games, we now make the players themselves the avatar in an AR basketball world to unlock rewards with sweats and efforts they paid in real world trainings

/ The Iterations

The Initial Attempt: A total gamification

Our first approach is to make a in-app avatar the represents the user. The user can dress the up according to their team preference and appearance.

👉 Avatars

These are the explorations of the avatar styles at the initial phase of the project. Trying to see if any style fits

👉 Locker Room

Locker room is an initial idea to contain the rewards and collectibles the players earned. Just like any NBA console gamess

/ Insights

'We don't need avatars as the players themselves are the avatars in the AR-world'

Unlike traditional gaming where the players control their own avatar to play the game, the players themselves are the avatars in the AR-world

Hence, the generally clean and professional UI of the Homecourt app won't be affected by the style of the avatars graphics

/ The Solution

A Simple In-app Store

After several rounds of iterations, the final verdict is to simply have an in-app store that can let players redeem collectibles with their earned tokens
